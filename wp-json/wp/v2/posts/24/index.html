LinkedIn, often deemed the essential channel for B2B marketers, holds a unique place in the realm of social media platforms. To master this platform and adapt to its evolving landscape, understanding its algorithm is paramount. This article delves into why LinkedIn should be an integral part of your marketing strategy, how a social media management tool can bolster your efforts, and how to navigate the LinkedIn algorithm in 2023.<\/p>\n

LinkedIn is often likened to Facebook’s more formal and professional cousin. While Instagram thrives on visuals, LinkedIn thrives on connections. Similar to Facebook, you can create a company page on LinkedIn to engage with your target audience.<\/p>\n

For advertisers, entrepreneurs, professionals, and business visionaries, LinkedIn is a haven of opportunities.<\/p>\n

One distinguishing factor sets LinkedIn apart from other social media platforms \u2013 its impressive organic reach. LinkedIn offers remarkable organic reach within a relatively short span.<\/p>\n

The LinkedIn Algorithm Unveiled<\/p>\n

LinkedIn may exude an air of sophistication and luxury, attracting businesses of all sizes, but beneath it all, it’s still a social media network.<\/p>\n

With a staggering 900 million users worldwide and counting, LinkedIn processes billions of posts. This mammoth task is undertaken to ensure you see content that matters to you. LinkedIn employs a clever algorithm to curate your feed, considering various factors such as your preferred topics, connections, and engagement patterns. This personalized approach keeps your feed intriguing and entices you to return for more valuable content.<\/p>\n

How Does the LinkedIn Algorithm Operate?<\/p>\n

While not identical to the Facebook algorithm, LinkedIn’s algorithm shares some similarities. LinkedIn follows a four-step process for content distribution across its network to minimize spam and inappropriate content. Understanding these steps is crucial when utilizing LinkedIn for content marketing.<\/p>\n

A Behind-the-Scenes Look at the LinkedIn Algorithm<\/p>\n

When you post an update on LinkedIn, several steps occur behind the scenes. The algorithm categorizes your content into one of three types, and the goal is to have it classified as “clear” to avoid spam. The algorithm then assesses your content’s credibility, usefulness, and relevance, which determines its fate and visibility.<\/p>\n

Initial Filter<\/strong><\/p>\n

Upon posting an update on LinkedIn, a bot immediately categorizes your content into one of three categories:<\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Spam<\/li>\n
  2. Low-quality<\/li>\n
  3. Clear<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    The ultimate aim is to land in the “clear” category. Even if your content is initially categorized as “low-quality,” there’s still hope; it might progress to the next stages.<\/p>\n

    Testing Phase<\/strong><\/p>\n

    Next comes the audience testing phase. After categorization, your post is shown to a small group of users to gauge its popularity. During this phase, your content must avoid being hidden by users or reported as spam.<\/p>\n

    Content Scoring<\/strong><\/p>\n

    Consider this phase akin to earning points for your Hogwarts house. After passing through the initial filters and audience testing, different interactions carry varying weights in the algorithm. For example, a “like” might score one point, while a comment scores two points, and a “share” scores three points. The overall score of your post determines its future \u2013 it can either be demoted for being low-quality or promoted for being high-quality.<\/p>\n

    Human Assessment<\/strong><\/p>\n

    If your post garners engagement during the previous phases, it proceeds to the last stage \u2013 human assessment by LinkedIn staff. They review each post to decide whether it should be shown to more people or if its journey ends there. If the content performs exceptionally well, it might even reach users outside of your immediate network, appearing in “trending content” or topical feeds.<\/p>\n

    A Quick Recap of LinkedIn Algorithm Ranking Signals<\/p>\n

    LinkedIn’s algorithm primarily relies on three key ranking signals to determine post order in users’ feeds:<\/p>\n

      \n
    1. Personal Connections:<\/strong> This signal emphasizes posts from users’ personal connections, focusing on interactions with connections and profiles with similar interests and skills.<\/li>\n
    2. Interest Relevance:<\/strong> LinkedIn values high relevance, analyzing user interests and aligning content accordingly to maximize interest relevance.<\/li>\n
    3. Engagement Probability:<\/strong> The algorithm assesses a user’s likelihood to engage with a post based on past interactions and content preferences, considering the speed of post engagement as well.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

      Mastering the LinkedIn Algorithm in 2023<\/p>\n

      Understanding the LinkedIn Algorithm need not be daunting. Trending posts with high engagement rates typically appear first on your LinkedIn feed. You can also sort posts by ‘Top’ or ‘Relevance.’<\/p>\n

      So, how does LinkedIn discern relevance? The answer is relatively straightforward. LinkedIn tailors content recommendations based on your recent activities, including comments, follows, and more.<\/p>\n

      Here are some tips to master the LinkedIn Algorithm in 2023:<\/p>\n

      Timing Is Crucial<\/strong><\/p>\n

      Imagine the scenario: the sun rises, and professionals begin their day with a cup of coffee, seeking inspiration and motivation. This is the perfect time to post thought-provoking content that resonates with them.<\/p>\n

      Alternatively, consider posting in the afternoon when minds are active, and people are engrossed in their work. The timing of your LinkedIn posts can significantly impact your overall engagement on the platform.<\/p>\n

      The Best Time to Post on LinkedIn in 2023<\/strong><\/p>\n

      Choosing the right time to post is paramount. Posting at odd hours, when most of your network is asleep, can result in your content going unnoticed, regardless of its quality. LinkedIn is a professional network, with most users active during typical working hours, from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.<\/p>\n

      Moreover, LinkedIn users often access the platform via mobile devices, allowing you to reach them even after work hours. While LinkedIn does not offer built-in content scheduling like some other platforms, you can leverage third-party LinkedIn scheduling tools to optimize your posting schedule.”<\/p>\n
